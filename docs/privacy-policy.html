Privacy Policy — Money Trail

Last updated: 3 July 2026

Money Trail ("the app") is an expense-tracking app that reads bank and payment SMS on your device to automatically organize your income and spending. This policy explains what data the app accesses, how it is used, and — most importantly — what is not done with it.

1. SMS Access

The app requests READ_SMS and RECEIVE_SMS permission to read bank/payment transaction messages (e.g. debit/credit alerts from your bank). This is the core function of the app: it parses these messages to detect amounts, merchants, and categorize your spending automatically.

  • All SMS parsing and categorization happens entirely on your device.
  • SMS content is never uploaded to our servers — we do not operate any server that receives your SMS data. There is no backend; the app has no company-run cloud component.
  • Only bank/transaction-pattern messages are processed; personal/social SMS are not specifically read or stored by the app's transaction list.

2. Notification Access (optional)

If you enable notification access, the app can also read bank transaction notifications (used for banks/UPI apps that send transaction alerts as notifications rather than SMS, e.g. RCS chat-based alerts). This is used only to detect the same kind of transaction data described above, processed on-device the same way.

3. Optional AI Categorization (Google Gemini)

The app includes an optional "AI Suggestions" feature. It is off by default and requires you to supply your own personal Google Gemini API key.

  • When enabled, the text of a transaction SMS — with the amount masked — is sent directly from your device to Google's Gemini API using your own API key, to suggest a spending category.
  • We (the app developer) do not see, receive, log, or store this data at any point. The request goes directly from your device to Google using your credentials.
  • You can disable this feature at any time from the app's Settings.

4. Optional Cloud Backup (Google Drive)

The app offers an optional backup feature. It is off until you sign in with your Google account.

  • If enabled, your app settings and categorized-transaction metadata (categories, tags, budgets, notes — not your raw SMS inbox) are saved to a private, hidden folder in your own Google Drive (Google's "Application Data" folder), which only this app can access and which does not appear in your regular Drive file list.
  • This data is not visible to us, is not stored on any server we operate, and is not shared with any third party. It stays entirely within your own Google account.
  • You can revoke this access at any time from your Google Account permissions page, or by signing out from within the app.

5. Biometric Authentication (optional)

If you enable an app lock, biometric verification (fingerprint/face) is handled entirely by Android's built-in BiometricPrompt system. Your biometric data never leaves your device's secure hardware and is never accessible to the app or to us.

6. Local Data Storage

All transaction, category, and budget data is stored in an encrypted local database on your device. Uninstalling the app permanently deletes this local data (unless you have a Google Drive backup enabled, in which case that backup remains in your own Drive until you delete it).

7. What We Don't Do

  • No advertising or ad-tracking SDKs are included in the app.
  • No analytics or crash-tracking SDKs collect or transmit usage data to us.
  • We do not sell, rent, or share your data with advertisers or data brokers.
  • We do not have a company server that stores your financial data — everything stays on your device or in your own Google account (Drive backup, Gemini API), under your control.

8. Permissions Summary

  • READ_SMS / RECEIVE_SMS — read transaction SMS for expense tracking (on-device only).
  • Notification access (optional) — read bank transaction notifications, same purpose.
  • Biometric — optional app-lock, handled by Android system APIs.
  • Internet — used only for the optional Gemini AI feature and the optional Google Drive backup feature, both user-initiated and user-controlled.

9. Children's Privacy

Money Trail is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect data from children.
